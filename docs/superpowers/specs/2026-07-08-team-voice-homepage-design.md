# Team-Voice Homepage Copy Pass

**Date:** 2026-07-08
**Status:** Approved for implementation
**Project:** Sentari Software Website

## Goal

Sharpen the live Sentari homepage so it sells the operational-mess offer more directly while presenting Sentari as a team-oriented software partner, not a solo freelancer.

## Approved Direction

Use the “messy process” framing:

- Main page CTA language: “Show us your messy process.”
- Cal.com call language: “Talk through your system.”
- Final CTA: “Send us the part of your operation that keeps slipping through the cracks.”

Keep the current visual design and overall page structure. This is a copy and emphasis pass, not a full visual redesign.

## Voice Rules

- Prefer “Sentari,” “we,” “us,” and “our.”
- Avoid making the homepage feel like “Cartrel alone with a laptop.”
- Keep founder credibility in a contained trust section:
  - “Founded by Cartrel James…”
  - Records-office discipline, secure systems, and operational rigor are valid proof.
- Do not overstate company size or invent staff, departments, or named team members.

## Content Changes

- Hero should make the pain concrete: organizations already run on a system, but it may be trapped in email, spreadsheets, and memory.
- The proof section should feel more like a before/after operating-system case study.
- “Why Cartrel” should become “Why Sentari.”
- Final section should invite the prospect to send the operational piece that keeps slipping.

## Constraints

- Static HTML only; no framework, build step, JavaScript, or dependency.
- Preserve the FormSubmit endpoint `https://formsubmit.co/info@sentarisoftware.com`.
- Preserve the Cal.com booking link `https://cal.com/cartrel-james-dwefpy/free-20-minute-call`.
- Keep public proof links:
  - `https://dashboard.sentarisoftware.com`
  - `https://demo.sentarisoftware.com`
- Keep public copy sanitized; do not expose raw private client names.
